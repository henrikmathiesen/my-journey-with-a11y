1 Perceivable
1.1 Text Alternatives
1.1.1 Non-text Content A Make information conveyed by non-text content accessible through the use of a text alternative. Text can be read visually, read out loud by assistive technology or be converted to braile. It helps: Visually impaired, hearing impaired. WCAG: Understanding 1.1.1 Non-text Content Short text alternative, Long text alternative, Alternatives for controls & inputs, Alternatives for time-based media, exams, or specific sensory experiences, CAPTCHA, Decorations or elements meant to be ignored by AT
1.2 Time-based Media
1.2.1 Audio-only and Video-only (Prerecorded) A Make information conveyed by non-text content accessible through the use of a text alternative. Text can be read visually, read out loud by assistive technology or be converted to braile. For prerecorded video content, authors have the option to provide an audio track. It helps: Visually impaired, hearing impaired, cognitive impaired(?) WCAG: Understanding 1.2.1 Audio-only and Video-only (Prerecorded) Long text alternative (text transcript), or provide a link to an audio version of the video-only presentation
1.2.2 Captions (Prerecorded) A The intent of this Success Criterion is to enable people who are deaf or hard of hearing to watch audio and video presentations. Captions are similar to dialogue-only subtitles except captions convey not only the content of spoken dialogue, but also equivalents for non-dialogue audio information needed to understand the program content, including sound effects, music, laughter, speaker identification and location. It helps: hearing impaired, cognitive impaired(?) WCAG: Understanding 1.2.2: Captions (Prerecorded) If you provide a video with sound, provide captions. The captions should include both speech and any other sounds that provide significant information.
1.2.3 Audio Description or Media Alternative (Prerecorded) A People who are blind or visually impaired should be able to access the visual information on audio and video presentation. It helps: Visually impaired, cognitive impaired(?) WCAG: Understanding 1.2.3: Audio Description or Media Alternative (Prerecorded) Long text alternative (text transcript), or a supplemental audio track, with audible explanation of what's happening on screen, placed during existing pauses in dialog
